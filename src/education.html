My Educational Journey: High School to University

Building Knowledge | Shaping Tomorrow

High School: The Foundation of My Academic Journey

My academic career was significantly shaped by my high school years. I was concentrating on gaining the discipline, problem-solving abilities, and resilience that would help me throughout my academic career. Along with extracurricular activities, the demanding curriculum taught me the value of tenacity and time management. Here, I developed the ability to strike a balance between my academic obligations and personal development, which was crucial when I entered college.

The National Higher Education Exam: A Turning Point

As we move from high school to university, it was marked by significant changes in the structure and expectations of the National Higher Education Entrance Exam. Once a simple activity, the exam evolved into a rough assessment that assessed not just knowledge but also a capacity for perseverance and performance under stress, and were new challenges for all of us as part of the first Bach to encounter this updated examination system. However I overcame this change with diligence, tenacity, and teamwork, demonstrating fortitude and laying the groundwork for my future academic success.

Addis Ababa University and My Path to AAiT

After successfully completing the National Exam, I entered Addis Ababa University, where my academic journey continued to unfold. During my freshman year, I was based at the 4Kilo campus, where I found the academic environment both stimulating and supportive. It was there that I developed a deeper understanding of my interests and aspirations. After carefully evaluating my options, I chose to pursue a degree at AAiT. This decision marked a new chapter in my educational journey, one that allowed me to explore my passion for technology and engineering.
